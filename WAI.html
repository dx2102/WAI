In my last WAI Assignment, I discussed the debate about whether AGI (Artificial general intelligence) is possible. I concluded that, large language models like GPT-4 can already do such a wide range of tasks, that it's not a problem to say that their intelligence is general. On the other hand, these models "think" in a very different way from humans. Just like airplanes and birds both fly, but they have very different principles. (Deming, 2022)

As I was reading the essay for assignment 2, I found the essay of Bubeck et al. (2023) more and more interesting. They published an article entitled "Sparks of Artificial Intelligence" Later, Bubeck also went to MIT and gave a presentation about this essay (2023). Bubeck made an interesting point in his speech. Usually when testing a machine learning model, people use some machine learning test set. For example, 1,000 multiple-choice questions on general knowledge of science can be counted as the test set. People then feed these questions to the model and measure the accuracy of the model's answers. But Bubeck said, "We're actually moving from machine learning to psychology... I don't want to use the machine learning test set. My assumption is that GPT-4 has seen any text on the Internet, including those tests... . What we have to do is be creative and ask it some questions that have never appeared on the Internet."
